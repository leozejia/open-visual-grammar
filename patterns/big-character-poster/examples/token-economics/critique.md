# Critique

This example belongs to `big-character-poster` because the title is the image's
main structure.

What matters:

- the first read comes from the dominant title;
- the conflict is a real operational warning;
- concrete evidence makes the pain real: cached input appears as a paid line
  item, not an abstract memory cloud;
- the visual action makes the correction visible: asset becomes liability;
- the title feels like a public warning, not a caption;
- details behave like evidence and texture, not like a full infographic;
- the cover has enough roughness to feel argued.

The score can compile into many different prompts. The prompt itself is not the
source of truth.
