# Editorial Illustration

Editorial Illustration is a canon for essays, narratives, and abstract technical
ideas where mood and metaphor matter more than immediate instruction.

## Use when

- The article is reflective or narrative.
- The topic needs human scale or atmosphere.
- The title does not fit a short poster phrase.
- The image should invite interpretation without becoming vague.

## Do not use when

- The reader needs exact UI proof.
- The channel requires a large readable claim.
- The page is a reference doc that should feel operational.
- The metaphor cannot be understood without long explanation.

## Visual problem

The viewer should feel the argument before reading it.

Editorial illustration turns a topic into a scene with emotional pressure.

## Invariants

- One metaphor.
- One emotional temperature.
- Strong composition over decorative detail.
- Human scale when useful.
- No generated text unless typeset manually later.
- Avoid generic AI symbols.

## Composition model

Use one of these models:

- Human facing system: a person, desk, or workspace meets an abstract force.
- Object as thesis: one object carries the argument.
- Atmosphere as state: weather, light, material, or room condition reveals the idea.
- Journey: a route, corridor, map, or threshold shows transition.

## Information density

Low to medium.

The viewer should not have to decode a diagram.

## Emotional temperature

Flexible, but deliberate.

Do not mix melancholy, urgency, wonder, and comedy in one image.

## Runtime compile slots

When compiling into an image prompt, define:

- essay argument;
- emotional temperature;
- metaphor distance;
- main subject;
- scene;
- composition;
- material texture;
- lighting;
- forbidden symbols and text.
