# Minimal Poster

Minimal Poster is a restrained canon for covers where one object, phrase, or
spatial relationship can carry the argument.

## Use when

- The topic needs premium calm.
- A single object can hold the metaphor.
- The channel does not require high conflict.
- The brand should feel confident rather than loud.

## Do not use when

- The reader needs step-by-step proof.
- The hook depends on a public argument.
- The concept is too complex for one object.
- The output would become empty abstraction.

## Visual problem

The viewer should feel: "There is one clear idea here."

Minimalism should sharpen the point, not hide the lack of one.

## Invariants

- One object or one typographic phrase.
- Strong whitespace.
- Limited palette.
- Precise spatial relationship.
- No decorative UI cards.
- No dense explanatory text.

## Composition model

Use one of these models:

- Isolated object: one object carries the metaphor.
- Imbalance: a small object under large negative space creates tension.
- Mark and field: a tiny mark changes the meaning of the whole surface.
- Typographic object: text behaves like material or structure.

## Information density

Low.

Every detail must earn its place.

## Emotional temperature

Quiet, cold, or warm depending on the subject.

Avoid accidental luxury cliche.

## Runtime compile slots

When compiling into an image prompt, define:

- core idea;
- single object or phrase;
- spatial relationship;
- palette;
- material;
- lighting;
- whitespace role;
- forbidden decoration.
