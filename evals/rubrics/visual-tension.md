# Rubric: Visual Tension

Visual tension is the visible relationship that gives the image a reason to
exist.

## Strong

- The image shows a collision, tradeoff, transformation, or handoff.
- The tension comes from the article's real point.
- The tension attacks a named false belief.
- The central metaphor performs a visible action.
- The visual metaphor and composition reinforce each other.
- The emotion is calibrated to the topic.

## Weak

- The image is only an attractive surface.
- The conflict is exaggerated beyond the article.
- The metaphor is unrelated to the viewer's problem.
- The metaphor is only an object, not an action.
- The composition has no meaningful pressure, direction, or hierarchy.

## Operator questions

- What two forces are in tension?
- What false belief is being challenged?
- What is the visual action?
- Is this tension honest?
- Does the composition make the tension visible?
- Would removing the tension leave only decoration?
