# Rubric: First Read

First read is what the viewer understands before inspecting details.

## Strong

- The main idea is visible within one second.
- The title, object, and composition point to the same idea.
- The image still communicates when downscaled.
- Supporting details reward inspection but are not required.

## Weak

- The viewer must read surrounding article text to understand the image.
- The strongest visual element is decorative.
- The title says one thing while the image says another.
- The first read depends on small text or exact UI labels.

## Operator questions

- What did I understand first?
- Is that the intended first read?
- Would a stranger get the same thing?
- Does the image still work at feed size?
