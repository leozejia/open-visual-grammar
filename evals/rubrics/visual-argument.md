# Rubric: Visual Argument

Visual argument tests whether the image has a cover-level claim before it has a
style.

## Strong

- The reader pain is concrete.
- The false belief is simple enough to challenge in one image.
- The correction is operational, not generic advice.
- The visual action is a verb that makes the correction visible.
- The metaphor performs the action instead of sitting beside the title.

## Weak

- The image starts from a topic and a style.
- The title is sharp but the image does not add a correction.
- The metaphor is a generic object that could fit many posts.
- The prompt lists facts without choosing one visible action.
- The cover looks good but the operator cannot say what belief it attacks.

## Operator Questions

- What false belief does this cover challenge?
- What correction does it make visible?
- What is the physical verb in the image?
- Could this visual action fit ten unrelated AI posts?
- Would the image still have a point if the title were removed?
