# Information Density

Information density is the amount of meaning the surface carries.

## Low density

One object, one phrase, or one atmospheric scene.

Use for:

- premium identity;
- one sharp claim;
- quiet covers;
- motion or material studies.

Risk: may become vague.

## Medium density

A few objects with visible relationships.

Use for:

- docs hero images;
- concept explanations;
- article covers that need trust;
- workflow illustrations.

Risk: can become generic if the relationships are not specific.

## High density

Many marks, objects, labels, or data-like surfaces.

Use only when density is part of the point:

- overload;
- cost accumulation;
- noisy context;
- crowded state;
- forensic proof.

Risk: the first read disappears.
